    Business Procedure¶

    1. Organization and cooperation management¶

    Business background: VARs are responsible for device distribution and organization management.

    They can create and manage organizations and add members but cannot manage departments or devices directly. Devices are managed only after being transferred to the organization.

    VAR administrators must create roles for device import/export operations and invite their employees for platform management.

    1.1. Creating or Modifying VAR Roles¶

    • When you enter the Role Management page, it will display the VAR Role Add and Edit page by default. To add a new role, click the Add a New Role button in the list. To modify an existing role, select the role you want to change and click Edit in the lower right corner. This action will open the role editing interface.

      ① Change Role Name: At the top right of the page, you can edit the role name.

      ② Set Role Privileges: In the right area, all functional modules of the VAR role are listed. You can control the corresponding operation privileges by toggling the buttons. Note that all functional modules must first have View privileges before other operational privileges can be adjusted.

    • To batch allow or disallow privileges, use the Allow All or Disable All buttons in the upper right corner.

    • After configuring the role, click the Apply button. The system will return to the role configuration page, and the new role will be created successfully.

    1.2. Inviting Members to Join the VAR¶

    Access the Members Page: Click on 'Members' in the left navigation bar. This page displays a list of members. VAR members are typically VAR employees responsible for managing transfers, configuring, and commissioning devices.

    Invite a Member: Click the 'Invite' button at the top of the list. The invitation process consists of two steps:

    • Step 1: Enter Invitation Details

      • Enter the email address or username of the user you want to invite in the pop-up window.

        • For Unregistered Emails: If the email is not registered, a pop-up will ask if you want to invite the user to register. Click 'Confirm' and 'Apply' to send an invitation email. The user must click the link in the email to register and be added to the VAR.

        • For Registered Emails: If the email is already registered, the system will display the user’s information.

      • Set the expiration date for the invitation (default is one hour; can range from one hour to 180 days).

      • Click 'Next'.

    • Step 2: Assign Roles and Permissions

      • Select the VAR role for this member in the next pop-up window. The role determines what the member can do with devices and data.

      • Choose the level of access:

        • 'None': No access to VAR subordinate organizations.

        • 'Partial Organizations': Allows configuration of specific organizations. Click '+ Add' to select the organizations the member can access and configure corresponding permissions.

          • After selecting the organizations, click 'Next' to return to the 'Member Settings' page.

          • Click 'Apply' to finalize the access settings.

        • 'All Organizations': If this option is selected, the invited employee will be granted access to all organizations under the VAR. Configure the unified permission roles and set a deadline for the employee’s access.

          • Please note that once this option is successfully configured, any new organization added by the VAR will automatically grant the employee the relevant rights based on the role permissions set at that time.

    • After configuring the access rights for this member, click the 'Apply' button to complete the invitation. This setup is typically used for VAR technical support staff, allowing them to access customer data and devices, and collaborate with customers to configure the devices.

    Other Actions:

    Invited members are listed on the right side of the page, with an "Unregistered" or "Unaccepted" status label next to their usernames. Once they complete the registration or authorization process by clicking the link in the email, their status will update accordingly. If a user accidentally deletes the email or clicks "Decline," you can select the member from the list and click "Re-Invite" to resend the invitation.

    To modify the VAR Roles for an invited member or adjust their roles related to VAR-affiliated organizations, click "Modify."

    2. VAR Devices Transfer Process (To Business Users)¶

    Business Background: After users purchase devices from a distributor (VAR), the distributor typically creates a separate organization for each member.

    For example, if Google buys routers from CSG, CSG will create an organization in GoodCloud named "Google Inc." The inventory is then allocated to the organization created by the distributor.

    2.1. Creating an Organization in the VAR¶

    • Access the Organization Page:

      • Click on 'Organizations' in the left navigation bar. The page will display a list of organizations created by this VAR, including basic details such as the organization's name, administrator, and number of devices. Note that devices and data in each organization are not interoperable.

    • Add a New Organization:

      • Click on the '+ Add' button. In the dialog box that appears, enter the name of the user's organization and click 'Next'.

    • Set Up an Administrator:

      • On the next page, enter the email address or username of the organization's administrator. The administrator should be a customer of the VAR, not an employee of the VAR. Set the expiration date for the invitation and click 'Next'.

    • Handle User Registration:

      • Depending on the user information entered:

        • If the email address is not registered: A prompt will ask if you want to invite the user to register. Click 'Confirm' and 'Apply'. The system will send an email to the user. They will join the organization after clicking the link in the email and completing the registration.

        • If the email address or username is already registered: The user information will be displayed. Click 'Apply'.

    • Complete the Organization Creation:

      • After successful creation, the new organization will appear in the list. As the VAR owner, you can select the organization and click 'Access' to enter the organization's view and manage it.

    2.2. Transfer devices¶

    • Filter and Search for Devices:

      • Use the search function in the upper right corner and advanced search options to locate the desired device.

        ① Fuzzy Matching: Device ID, MAC, SN, storage warehouse, and order number; Advanced Search: Allows logical customized matching.

        ② Selection: Choose matching conditions (all or any).

        ③ Add Condition: Up to five conditions can be added.

        ④ Matching Logic: Comprises matching parameters, logical relationships, and search contents.

        ⑤ Apply: Results must satisfy all specified matching logic.

      • Searches are generally conducted in the following MAC filter form:

    • Transfer Devices:

      ① Select Devices: Choose the filtered devices.

      ② Click 'Transfer': Initiate the transfer process.

      ③ Select Customer Organization: Choose the organization to receive the devices from the pop-up window.

      ④ Apply: Complete the transfer.

    • View Outgoing Devices:

      • Switch to Organization View:

        • From Home Page: Click on Online Devices, Offline Devices, Inactive Devices, or Total Devices to view.

        • Navigation Bar: Go to the 'Devices' menu on the left.

    Note: Organizations created under the VAR cannot add devices on their own. They can only receive and manage devices transferred by the VAR.

    3. VAR Devices Transfer Process (To Individual Users)¶

    Business Background: For individual users, the VAR creates a single organization with its administrators being VAR employees.

    For instance, if a device is sold to an individual user, the device is transferred to this organization (e.g., Organization A) and pre-configured. The individual user then binds the device to the cloud platform upon receipt.

    4. Configuring Device Brand Information (White Label of Firmware)¶

    Business Background: Since the logo on the Admin Panel cannot be preset during production, white-label users need to configure the logo and other brand information through the firmware management Admin Panel on GoodCloud.

    4.1. Modify Device Web-GUI Information¶

    • In the VAR view, navigate to the Settings Template page.

    • Click on "Modify" at the top.

    • Toggle on "Enable Brand Settings Template" in the pop-up window. This will allow GoodCloud to automatically apply brand information (e.g., trademarks) to the firmware Admin Panel for all new devices that come online and belong to the VAR.

    • Enter the required information, including:

      • Domain name;

      • Company name;

      • Company website;

      • Logo;

      • Other relevant details.

    • Click "Confirm" to save the configuration.

    • Once configured, the top card will indicate that the brand configuration template is enabled. In this state, organizations under the VAR will not be able to configure their own brand information.

    5. Configuring GoodCloud Brand Information (White Label of GoodCloud)¶

    Business background: For White Label business only. Custom domain name configuration details can be found in the https://static.goodcloud.xyz/docs/WhiteLabel_Domain.pdf. Contact us if you need further assistance.

    5.1. Modify Cloud Platform Interface Information¶

    Steps:

    • Review the White Label Domain Guide for detailed instructions on customizing your domain name.

    • Contact GoodCloud support if you require assistance with configuring your White Label brand information or have specific needs related to the branding process.

    • Follow the provided guide to set up your domain name and other brand details as required for your White Label setup.
